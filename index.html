Guidance Notes

▼

Why this balance?

About 58% of your weekly time is strength-focused (lower body, upper body, full body/pilates) and 42% is cardio and core. This ratio is intentional: for gradual, healthy weight loss, preserving and building lean muscle is more effective than heavy cardio. Muscle tissue burns more calories at rest, and resistance training is what creates the lean, toned look rather than just a lower number on the scale.

Sequencing tips

Avoid back-to-back lower body days. Your legs and glutes need at least one day to recover between strength sessions. If you did a leg session yesterday, pick cardio, upper body, or core today.

Pair a strength day with a lighter day. After a tough full body or lower body session, a walking cardio or core session the next day allows recovery while keeping momentum.

Upper body can go almost anywhere. Arms and shoulders recover quickly and don't interfere much with other session types, so slot these wherever convenient.

Splitting is fine. A 45-minute target can be two 20-minute sessions, or a 30 and a 15. What matters is the weekly total per category, not individual session length.

A note on progression

If this balance starts feeling easy after a few weeks, the first thing to increase is the intensity of your strength sessions (heavier weights, harder video progressions) rather than adding more time. Quality over quantity.

What about rest days?

With 3 hours spread across 7 days, you'll naturally have lighter days or full rest days. That's by design. Recovery is when your muscles actually adapt and get stronger. Aim for at least one full rest day per week, two if your body asks for it.
